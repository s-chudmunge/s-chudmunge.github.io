Research Interests

My background in computational physics has given me strong foundations in numerical methods, Fourier analysis, and mathematical modeling. Through my master's research on quantum systems, I developed skills in signal processing, solving inverse problems, and working with complex algorithms. I'm now drawn to electrical and computer engineering because it lets me apply these skills to real-world problems.

I'm particularly interested in medical imaging, where MRI's k-space formulation and reconstruction algorithms mirror the mathematical tools I used in quantum mechanics. The field combines electromagnetic theory, signal processing, and inverse problems in ways that feel natural to my physics background. Beyond imaging, I want to explore how the wave phenomena and frequency analysis from physics translate to communication systems, RF design, and signal processing applications. Machine learning also excites me as a way to extend the optimization and pattern recognition methods I learned in computational physics to solve engineering problems in imaging and signal processing.

What drives me is using mathematical and computational tools to address tangible challenges in medical diagnostics, communications, and intelligent systems.
