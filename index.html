Hey there! I'm fascinated by the weird and wonderful world of quantum many-body systems and computational physics. You know how sometimes when you put a bunch of particles together, they start doing completely unexpected things? That's what gets me excited! My work dives into understanding how quantum entanglement between many particles creates these mind-blowing emergent phenomena like topological states and Majorana zero modes.

Right now, I'm deep into studying Majorana Zero Edge States in Quantum Ising Chains with Longer-Range Interactions (quite a mouthful, I know!). What's really cool is how adding these three-spin interaction terms suddenly creates these incredibly rich phase diagrams with multiple phase transitions and critical points. It's like watching a quantum soap opera unfold, with topologically protected edge states emerging as the main characters.

When it comes to actually figuring all this out, I'm all about the computational approach. I use numerical diagonalization, Jordan-Wigner transformations, and Bogoliubov-de Gennes methods to decode the collective behavior of these quantum systems. There's something almost magical about how quantum operators, entanglement, and topological invariants dance together to create these fascinating phase transitions.

My computational toolkit includes everything from Python and NumPy for algorithmic modeling to Jordan-Wigner transformations and BdG diagonalization for quantum many-body theory. I also spend a lot of time calculating winding numbers and mapping phase diagrams for topological analysis, plus diving into critical point analysis and quantum phase transitions from a statistical mechanics perspective. It's like being a quantum detective, but with more math and fewer magnifying glasses!
